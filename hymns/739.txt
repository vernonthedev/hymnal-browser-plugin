[Slide 1]
Now the point in what we are saying is this: we have such a high priest, one who is seated at the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in heaven, a minister in the sanctuary and the true tent which is set up not by man but by the Lord.
[Slide 2]
For every high priest is appointed to offer gifts and sacrifices; hence it is necessary for this priest also to have something to offer
[Slide 3]
Now if he were on earth, the would not be a priest all, since there are priests who offer gifts according to the law.
[Slide 4]
But when Christ appeared as a high priest Of the good things that have come then through the greater and more perfect tent (not made with hands, that is, not of this creation) he entered once for all into the Holy Place, taking not the blood of goats and calves but his own blood, thus securing an eternal redemption.
[Slide 5]
For Christ has entered not into a sanctuary made with hands, a copy of the true one, but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God on our behalf.
[Slide 6]
Nor was it to offer himself repeatedly, as the high priest enters the Holy Place yearly with blood not his own; for then he would have had to suffer repeatedly since the foundation of the world.
[Slide 7]
But as it is, he has appeared once for all at the end of the age to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself.
[Slide 8]
And just as it is appointed for men to die once, and after that comes judgment, so Christ, having been offered once to bear the sins of many, will appear a second time, not to deal with sin but to save those who are eagerly waiting fro him.
