[Slide 1]
“Keep my Sabbaths holy, that they may be a sign between us. Then you will know that I am the Lord your God.”
[Slide 2]
“[The Sabbath] will be a sign between me and the Israelites forever, for in six days the Lord made the heavens and the earth, and on the seventh day he abstained from work and rested.”
[Slide 3]
“As the new heavens and the new earth that I make will endure before me,” declares the Lord, “so will your name and descendants endure.
[Slide 4]
“From one New Moon to another and from one Sabbath to another, all mankind will come and bow down before me,” says the Lord.
[Slide 5]
“If you keep your feet from breaking the Sabbath and from doing as you please on my holy day, if you call the Sabbath a delight and the Lord’s holy day honourable, and if you honor it by not going your own way and not doing as you please or speaking idle words,
[Slide 6]
then you will find your joy in the Lord, and I will cause you to ride on the heights of the land and to feast on the inheritance of your father Jacob.” The mouth of the Lord has spoken.
[Slide 7]
[Jesus] went top Nazareth, where he had been brought up, and on the Sabbath day he went into the synagogue, as was his custom.
[Slide 8]
He said to them, “If any of you has a sheep and it falls into a pit on the Sabbath, will you not take hold of it and lift it out?  How much more valuable is a man than a sheep! Therefore it is lawful to do good on the Sabbath!”
