[Slide 1]
Though I speak with the tongues of men and of angels, but have not love I have become as sounding brass or a clanging cymbal.
[Slide 2]
And though I have the gift of prophecy, and understand all mysteries and all knowledge, and though I have all faith so that I could remove mountains, but have not love I am nothing.
[Slide 3]
And though I bestow all my goods to feed the poor, and though I give my body to be burned, but have not love it profits me nothing.
[Slide 4]
Love suffers long and is kind; love does not envy; love does not parade itself, Is not puffed up;
[Slide 5]
does not behave rudely, does not seek its own, is not provoked, thinks no evil;
[Slide 6]
does not rejoice in iniquity, but rejoices in the truth; bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.
[Slide 7]
Love never fails. But whether there are prophecies, they will fail; whether there are tongues, they will cease; whether there is knowledge, it will vanish away.
[Slide 8]
For we know in part and we prophesy in part. But when that which is perfect has come, then that which is in part will be done away.
[Slide 9]
When I was a child, I spoke as a child, I understood as a child, I though as a child; but when I became a man, I put away childish things.
[Slide 10]
For now we see in a mirror, dimly, but then face to face. Now I know in part, but then I shall know just as I also m known.
[Slide 11]
And now abide faith, hope. Love, these three; but the greatest of these is love.
